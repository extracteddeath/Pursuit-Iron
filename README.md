Pursuit Iron — Strength Coach
Pursuit Iron builds you a training program, then keeps adjusting it as you lift. Tell it your goal, how many days a week you can train, how long each session can run, and what equipment you actually have — a full gym, a garage rack, or a pair of dumbbells — and it generates a complete week from a library of around 350 exercises. You can start from a proven template instead (5/3/1, nSuns, GZCLP, Madcow, Texas Method, GZCL's Rippler and Jacked & Tan), or chain blocks into a periodized cycle that moves you through accumulation, intensification and a peak.
What separates it from a logger is that it reads what you log. Weekly volume per muscle is tracked against MEV/MAV/MRV landmarks, and it will tell you when a muscle is under-dosed or past what you're recovering from. Loads are suggested from your own estimated 1RM and the reps-in-reserve you report, so the prescription tracks your actual performance rather than a fixed percentage table. It watches for genuine plateaus, distinguishes a strength ceiling from accumulated fatigue, and earns a deload from evidence in your training rather than a date on a calendar. Beginners get simple linear progression until they stall out of it, because that's what works; heavy compounds are kept away from failure, because grinding them there is mostly junk fatigue.
The design principle throughout is that it should only tell you things it can support. Volume advice cites the set counts it rests on. The dose-response model that estimates your personal productive volume range stays silent unless your history actually contains enough spread to identify one — a group average dressed up as a personal finding would be worse than no answer. That restraint is why the coaching is worth reading.
Day to day it's a fast, quiet logging app: rest timers, a plate calculator, warm-up ramps, supersets, and notes pinned to a lift that resurface every time it comes up — including the rack settings you'd otherwise re-derive every session, like the J-hook height and seat position. It's a PWA, so it installs to your home screen and works completely offline in a basement gym with no signal. Your data stays on your device — no account, no server, no ads, no subscription — and syncs between your own devices through an export you control, with a merge that treats your training history as something it must never lose.
